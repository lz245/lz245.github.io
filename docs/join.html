We Are Recruiting!

The Zhang Lab is actively seeking motivated students and postdocs to join our team. We particularly welcome applications from individuals with diverse backgrounds and perspectives.

Why Join Us?

Cutting-Edge Research

Work with the latest sequencing technologies and computational methods to solve real-world problems in food safety and public health.

Supportive Environment

Join a collaborative lab culture that prioritizes mentorship, work-life balance, and professional development.

Career Preparation

Develop skills valued in academia, government (CDC, USDA, FDA), and industry through hands-on training and networking opportunities.

Computational Focus

Gain expertise in bioinformatics, data science, and reproducible research practices that are increasingly essential in biology.

What We’re Looking For

Beyond specific qualifications, successful lab members share these qualities:

✓ Curiosity — Genuine interest in understanding how things work
✓ Persistence — Willingness to troubleshoot and iterate
✓ Communication — Ability to explain your work clearly
✓ Collaboration — Comfort working in a team environment
✓ Integrity — Commitment to honest, rigorous science
✓ Growth mindset — Eagerness to learn new skills

We do not expect you to already be an expert. We will teach you what you need to know.

About Mississippi State

Mississippi State University is a land-grant research university located in Starkville, Mississippi. With strong programs in agriculture, engineering, and the sciences, MSU offers excellent resources for graduate training.

Starkville is a friendly college town with affordable cost of living, mild winters, and a welcoming community. The campus is about 2 hours from Jackson, 3 hours from Memphis, and 4 hours from Birmingham.

FAQ

Q: Do I need programming experience to join the lab?
A: Not necessarily! For graduate students, we can teach you what you need. However, demonstrating willingness to learn (e.g., taking a free online course) strengthens your application.

Q: Is the GRE required?
A: No. MSU does not require the GRE for most graduate programs.

Q: Can international students apply?
A: Yes! We welcome international applicants. MSU’s Office of International Services can assist with visa requirements.

Q: What if I’m interested but not sure about a specific project?
A: That’s fine! Email Dr. Li to discuss your interests and we can identify good fits.

Q: How long does the application process take?
A: After initial contact, expect 2-4 weeks for an initial response. Graduate admissions decisions are typically made in February-March for fall admission.

Q: Is there funding for conference travel?
A: Yes. Graduate students are supported to attend at least one conference per year. Additional travel funding is available for presentations.

How to Apply

Step 1: Initial Contact

Email Dr. Li at l.zhang@msstate.edu with:

  • Subject line: “[Position Type] Inquiry - [Your Name]”
  • Brief introduction (1 paragraph)
  • Why you’re interested in our research (1 paragraph)
  • Attached CV/resume

Step 2: Discussion

If there’s a potential fit, we’ll schedule a video call to discuss your background, interests, and goals.

Step 3: Formal Application

For graduate positions, submit formal application through MSU Graduate School. For postdocs, provide full application materials.

Step 4: Interview

Invited candidates will interview (virtual or in-person) with Dr. Li and lab members.

Tips for Strong Applications
  • Be specific about why our lab interests you (not just generic statements)
  • Show initiative — Have you taken relevant courses? Done independent learning?
  • Demonstrate fit — Explain how your goals align with our research
  • Proofread — Attention to detail matters

Questions not answered here? Email l.zhang@msstate.edu — I try to respond within one week.
