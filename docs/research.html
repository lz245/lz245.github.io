Research Programs

Computational and molecular approaches to poultry health and food safety

Our research integrates molecular biology, genomics, and microbiology to address fundamental questions in poultry health and food safety. We develop methods that are both scientifically rigorous and practically applicable to real-world surveillance and disease prevention.

Program 1: Avian Pathogenic E. coli (APEC) Pathogenesis & Vaccines

Overview

Avian pathogenic Escherichia coli (APEC) causes colibacillosis, resulting in significant economic losses to the poultry industry worldwide. We apply reverse vaccinology, computational immunoinformatics, and molecular characterization to identify novel vaccine candidates and understand host-pathogen interactions.

Current Projects

Reverse Vaccinology for Universal Vaccine Development

  • Identified conserved surface-exposed proteins across diverse APEC lineages using pangenome analysis
  • Discovered pagP as a potential universal vaccine candidate through host-pathogen interaction analysis
  • Validated vaccine candidates using HD11 macrophage cell line infection models
  • Collaborating with USDA ARS for in vivo challenge studies

High-Throughput APEC Characterization

  • Developed Oxford Nanopore sequencing-based MLST (multilocus sequence typing) pipeline
  • Characterized >500 APEC isolates from Mississippi broiler operations
  • Complete genome sequencing of multidrug-resistant APEC strains
  • Phenotypic virulence characterization using embryo lethality assays

Bioluminescent Imaging for Pathogenesis Studies

  • Constructed bioluminescent APEC strains for real-time infection tracking
  • Evaluating transmission dynamics and tissue colonization patterns

Funding

  • USDA ARS (2025–2026): “Immunological Characteristics of Poultry Vaccinated/Challenged with APEC” — $184,269 (PI)
  • USDA ARS (2024–2029): “Characterization of APEC Field Isolates and Vaccine Targets” — $317,000/year (Co-PI)
  • USDA ARS (2024–2028): “Integrating Genomics, Epidemiology, and Diagnostics” — $274,480 (PI)

Key Publications

  • Jia et al. (2025) Journal of Applied Microbiology — pagP vaccine candidate identification
  • Jia et al. (2024) Poultry Science — High-throughput MLST analysis
  • Jia et al. (2024) Microbiology Resource Announcements — Complete genome sequences
  • Li et al. (2021) Poultry Science — In ovo probiotic effects on APEC incidence

Program 2: Campylobacter jejuni Control & Vaccine Development

Overview

Campylobacter jejuni is a leading cause of bacterial foodborne illness worldwide. We focus on understanding prevalence patterns, antimicrobial resistance, and developing effective vaccines to reduce colonization in poultry—the primary reservoir for human infections.

Current Projects

Vaccine Candidate Identification

  • In silico prediction of novel vaccine candidates using reverse vaccinology
  • Expression analysis of candidate genes during avian cell line infection
  • Identified BtuB-based immune intervention strategies
  • Developing cytokine response profiles for vaccine efficacy assessment

Prevalence & Antimicrobial Resistance Surveillance

  • Characterized C. jejuni from “No Antibiotics Ever” (NAE) broiler operations
  • Complete genome sequencing of isolates revealing AMR and virulence gene profiles
  • Aerotolerance studies linking survival mechanisms to food safety risks
  • Multi-locus sequence typing for epidemiological tracking

Biofilm & Survival Mechanisms

  • Temperature effects on biofilm formation under aerobic and microaerobic conditions
  • Role of biofilms in processing plant contamination
  • Developing intervention strategies based on survival mechanisms

Funding

  • MSU MAFES (2023–2025): “Universal Vaccine Against C. jejuni Colonization in Poultry” — $100,000 (PI)
  • USPoultry (2020–2021): “Vaccine Candidates for C. jejuni” — $55,000 (PI)
  • MSU MAFES (2019): “Prevalence of Campylobacter in Mississippi Poultry” — $49,400 (PI)

Key Publications

  • Poudel et al. (2024) Poultry Science — NAE broiler prevalence and AMR
  • Poudel et al. (2024) World Poultry Science Journal — Vaccine development review

Program 3: Rapid Pathogen Detection & Diagnostics

Overview

Early and accurate detection of poultry pathogens is critical for disease management. We develop field-deployable molecular diagnostic tools including LAMP (loop-mediated isothermal amplification) assays and real-time PCR methods for rapid, on-site pathogen detection.

Current Projects

LAMP Assay Development for Clostridium perfringens

  • Optimized LAMP assay for detecting toxigenic C. perfringens causing necrotic enteritis
  • Evaluated multiple DNA extraction methods for field compatibility
  • Developing portable device protocols for on-farm use
  • Target: Field-deployable quantitative detection system

Multiplex Real-Time PCR for APEC

  • Developed probe-based multiplex qPCR for rapid APEC detection
  • TaqMan assays for profiling pro-inflammatory cytokine responses during infection
  • Validation across diverse sample types (tissue, litter, environmental)

Oxford Nanopore Sequencing Applications

  • High-throughput serotyping and MLST workflows
  • Species-level microbiome characterization using long-read amplicon sequencing
  • Real-time sequencing protocols for outbreak investigation

Funding

  • MSU MAFES (2021–2022): “Field-Deployable Detection System for C. perfringens” — $49,900 (PI)
  • MSU MAFES (2021): “Novel LAMP Assay for Rapid C. perfringens Detection” — $49,800 (PI)

Key Publications

  • Chaudhary et al. (2024) Journal of Applied Poultry Research — LAMP assay optimization
  • Chaudhary et al. (2025) Journal of Applied Poultry Research — DNA extraction methods
  • Ramachandran et al. (2021) — Multiplex real-time PCR validation

Program 4: Poultry Gut Microbiome & Health

Overview

The gut microbiome plays critical roles in poultry health, disease resistance, and production efficiency. We apply culturomics and metagenomic approaches to characterize microbial communities and identify beneficial bacteria for probiotic development.

Current Projects

Culturomics for Probiotic Discovery

  • Targeted cultivation and isolation of probiotic candidates from broiler ceca
  • Genomic characterization of beneficial bacterial isolates
  • Evaluating probiotic effects on pathogen colonization resistance
  • Developing core beneficial microbiome profiles for enhanced gut health

Microbiome-Disease Associations

  • Cecal microbiota contributions to woody breast myopathy development
  • Gut microbiome changes associated with Eimeria challenge
  • Effects of dietary interventions (riboflavin, Bacillus subtilis) on microbiome composition

Spoilage Bacteria Characterization

  • Culture-free metagenomic analysis of chicken and catfish spoilage bacteria
  • Full-length 16S rRNA nanopore sequencing for species-level identification
  • Microbiome dynamics during refrigerated storage

Funding

  • USDA ARS (2024–2025): “Catfish Spoilage Bacteria Culturomics” — $55,000 (PI)

Program 5: Woody Breast Myopathy Research

Overview

Woody breast myopathy is a significant quality defect in commercial broilers causing substantial economic losses. We investigate the molecular mechanisms, gut microbiome associations, and nutritional interventions to understand and mitigate this condition.

Current Projects

Gene Expression & Metabolic Pathways

  • Differential expression of fatty acid metabolism genes in affected breast muscle
  • Inflammation pathway involvement in woody breast development
  • Changes in intestinal mucus gene expression
  • Integrating transcriptomics with phenotypic characterization

Microbiome-Myopathy Connections

  • Cecal microbiota differences between affected and unaffected birds
  • Effects of dietary bacitracin and Bacillus subtilis on myopathy incidence
  • Gut health interventions to reduce woody breast occurrence

Nutritional Interventions

  • Riboflavin and probiotic supplementation effects
  • Internal organ and skeletal muscle development patterns
  • Eimeria challenge interactions with woody breast incidence

Key Publications

  • Jia et al. (2025) Avian Diseases — Fatty acid metabolism gene expression
  • Jia et al. (2024) Poultry Science — Intestinal mucus gene expression changes
  • Zhang & Zhang et al. (2021) Poultry Science — Cecal microbiota contributions
  • Jia et al. (2022) Poultry Science — Dietary interventions and Eimeria challenge
  • Jia et al. (2022) Poultry Science — Internal organ development patterns

Program 6: Salmonella & Food Safety

Overview

Salmonella contamination remains a major food safety concern in poultry production. We investigate transmission dynamics, biofilm formation, and environmental survival to develop effective control strategies.

Current Projects

Vertical Transmission in Turkeys

  • Constructed bioluminescent Salmonella Reading strains for tracking
  • Evaluating vertical transmission potential in broiler breeders
  • Real-time imaging of colonization patterns

Biofilm Formation & Environmental Persistence

  • Biofilm development on processing plant surfaces under fluidic shear stress
  • Expression of biofilm-associated genes under environmental stress
  • Surface material effects on Salmonella biofilm formation

Funding

Key Publications
