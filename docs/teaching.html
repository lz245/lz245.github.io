Teaching & Mentoring

Courses, workshops, and student mentorship

Teaching Philosophy

I am committed to training the next generation of scientists in both molecular and computational approaches to poultry science and food safety. My teaching emphasizes hands-on laboratory skills, reproducible methods, and real-world applications of molecular biology and microbiome analysis.

Current Courses

PO/ADS 8123: Advanced Techniques in Agricultural Microbiome Analysis

Level: Graduate (Ph.D./M.S.)
Credits: 3
Semester: Fall (2024, 2025)
Format: Lecture + Laboratory

Description: This advanced course covers cutting-edge computational and molecular methods for analyzing microbiome data in agricultural systems. Students learn 16S rRNA analysis, shotgun metagenomics, culturomics approaches, and nanopore sequencing-based characterization methods.

Learning Objectives:

  • Design and implement microbiome studies in poultry and agricultural contexts
  • Process and analyze 16S rRNA and metagenomic sequence data
  • Apply culturomics approaches for bacterial isolation and characterization
  • Critically evaluate microbiome literature and experimental designs

Prerequisites: Graduate standing; basic knowledge of microbiology

PO/FNH 4512/6512: Poultry Product Safety & Sanitation

Level: Undergraduate/Graduate
Credits: 3
Semester: Spring (2021–2025)
Format: Lecture + Laboratory
Student Rating: 4.8/5.0

Description: Comprehensive coverage of food safety principles in poultry production and processing. Topics include HACCP, pathogen control, sanitation procedures, regulatory compliance, and emerging food safety technologies.

Topics Include:

  • HACCP principles and implementation
  • Pathogen biology (Salmonella, Campylobacter, Listeria, E. coli)
  • Sanitation and antimicrobial interventions
  • FDA/USDA regulatory framework
  • Emerging detection technologies

Prerequisites: Junior standing or instructor permission

PO 4514/6514: Poultry Processing

Level: Undergraduate/Graduate
Credits: 3
Semester: Fall 2023
Format: Lecture + Plant Visits

Description: Overview of modern poultry processing operations from live receiving through further processing. Emphasis on food safety integration throughout the production chain.

Topics Include:

  • Processing plant operations and flow
  • Slaughter and evisceration procedures
  • Chilling and antimicrobial treatments
  • Cut-up and deboning operations
  • Quality control and food safety

PO 8990: Molecular Techniques in Poultry Research

Level: Graduate
Credits: Variable
Semester: Fall (2019, 2022)
Format: Laboratory-intensive

Description: Hands-on training in molecular biology techniques commonly used in poultry research. Students gain practical experience with DNA/RNA extraction, PCR, qPCR, sequencing library preparation, and data analysis.

Techniques Covered:

  • DNA/RNA extraction from diverse sample types
  • Conventional and real-time PCR optimization
  • LAMP assay development
  • Nanopore sequencing library preparation
  • Bioinformatics analysis pipelines

Mentoring Philosophy

My approach to mentoring includes:

  1. Individual Development Plans (IDPs) — All graduate students create and regularly update career development plans
  2. Weekly one-on-one meetings — Regular meetings to discuss research progress and challenges
  3. Professional development — Support for conference attendance, scientific writing, and career exploration
  4. Hands-on training — As a molecular biologist, I personally train students in laboratory techniques
  5. Collaborative environment — Lab members support each other and contribute to departmental activities
  6. Work-life balance — Expectations that respect personal well-being

Laboratory Training

I provide hands-on training to graduate students throughout the department in molecular biology techniques including:
